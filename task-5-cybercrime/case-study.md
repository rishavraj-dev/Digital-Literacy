# Case Study: Ransomware (AIIMS Delhi Cyberattack)

## What the crime is:
In November 2022, AIIMS Delhi—one of India's biggest and most important hospitals—was hit by a massive ransomware attack. Hackers managed to lock down the hospital's main servers, blocking doctors and staff from accessing patient data, and demanded a huge ransom in cryptocurrency to give the access back.

## How it typically happens (step-by-step):
1. **System Breach:** Hackers find a weak spot in the network, like an outdated server or a compromised staff password, to sneak into the system.
2. **Spreading Inside:** Once inside, they quietly move through the hospital's network to find the most important databases and turn off any backup servers.
3. **Locking the Data:** They launch the ransomware virus, which instantly scrambles and locks digital patient records, appointment systems, and the main e-hospital network.
4. **The Demand:** A message pops up on the computers demanding payment in exchange for the digital key needed to unlock the files.

## Who is usually targeted:
Even though anyone can accidentally download ransomware, these highly organized attacks usually target big institutions like hospitals, universities, or government offices. Hackers know these places hold critical data and will be under extreme pressure to pay up quickly so they can get back to normal operations.

## What the consequences are:
The fallout for AIIMS was huge. The hospital had to completely shut down its online systems and go back to using pen and paper for almost everything. This caused terrible delays for patients needing surgeries and emergency care. On top of that, the private medical records of millions of patients were exposed, making it a massive privacy disaster.
