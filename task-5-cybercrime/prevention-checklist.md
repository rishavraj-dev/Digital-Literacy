# 8-Point "Stay Safe Online" Prevention Checklist

### Financial & UPI Safety
1. **Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money:** A UPI PIN is only required when you are *sending* money or checking your balance, never to accept a payment or claim a refund.
2. **Verify the receiver before paying:** Always double-check the registered name and UPI ID that pops up on your screen before hitting "Send," and decline random payment requests from unknown numbers.
3. **Beware of urgent "Account Blocked" messages:** Banks and digital wallets will never send you an SMS with a random link asking you to update your PAN or KYC details to unblock an account. 

### General Digital Security
4. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Turn on 2FA for all your primary accounts (email, banking, and social media) so hackers cannot log in even if they get your password.
5. **Avoid public Wi-Fi for banking:** Never make financial transactions or log into sensitive portals while connected to unsecured public Wi-Fi (like at cafes or railway stations) unless you are using a trusted VPN.
6. **Keep your software updated:** Regularly update your phone's operating system and banking apps, as these updates contain critical patches for newly discovered security flaws.
7. **Use strong, unique passwords:** Avoid using the same password for your college portal and your bank account. Use a password manager to keep track of complex passwords.

### Emergency Action
8. **Report cybercrime immediately:** If you fall victim to an online scam or financial fraud, immediately dial the National Cyber Crime Helpline at **1930** or file a formal complaint at the official portal: **[cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in/)**.
